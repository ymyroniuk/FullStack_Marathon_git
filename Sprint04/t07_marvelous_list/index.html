Public utilities are on strike in the city, unemployment reigns. A loser comedian named Arthur Fleck lives with his sick mother Penny. He works as a clown in a small entertainment agency and dreams of becoming a stand-up comedian. His idol is the popular TV presenter Murray Franklin, whose evening show Arthur and his mother watch every day. The failed comedian even imagines how well their meeting in reality could have been. In addition, Arthur suffers from a neurological disorder that makes him laugh in stressful situations, requiring him to take strong prescription drugs from a social worker.
